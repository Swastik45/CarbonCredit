üåø What is NDVI?

Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) is a standardized index that quantifies vegetation health, density, and photosynthetic activity using satellite remote sensing data.

Developed in the early 1970s, NDVI has become the most widely used vegetation index in remote sensing and environmental monitoring. It exploits the fact that healthy vegetation strongly reflects near-infrared light while absorbing red light for photosynthesis.

The Science Behind NDVI

Vegetation appears green to our eyes because it reflects green light while absorbing red and blue light for photosynthesis. However, healthy leaves also reflect a significant amount of near-infrared (NIR) light, which is invisible to humans but detectable by satellites.

By comparing the reflectance in the red and near-infrared portions of the electromagnetic spectrum, NDVI provides a measure of vegetation vigor and density.

üßÆ How NDVI is Calculated

The NDVI Formula

NDVI = (NIR - RED) / (NIR + RED)

NIR (Near-Infrared)

Reflectance in near-infrared band (typically 0.7-1.1 Œºm)

Healthy vegetation: High reflectance (0.4-0.6)

RED (Red)

Reflectance in red band (typically 0.6-0.7 Œºm)

Healthy vegetation: Low reflectance (0.05-0.10)
